> Access a curated library of 140 proven marketing approaches — filtered by growth stage, channel, and team size.

## What This Skill Does

**The Challenge**: Marketing ideation often circles the same familiar tactics: blog posts, paid ads, cold outreach. Teams miss high-leverage opportunities in channels they haven't considered or approaches that work specifically for their stage.

**The Solution**: Marketing Ideas skill provides a structured library of 140 proven approaches for SaaS and software products, organized by growth stage, channel, audience type, and execution complexity. Filters to the most relevant ideas for your specific situation.

## Activation

**Implicit**: Activates when user asks for marketing ideas, growth ideas, or channel suggestions.

**Explicit**: Activate via prompt:
```
Activate marketing-ideas skill to generate ideas for [product/stage/goal]
```

### 2. Stage Filter
Match ideas to your current growth stage.

**Stages and priorities**:
- **Pre-launch** (0 customers): Waitlist building, beta recruitment, community seeding
- **Early traction** (1-100 customers): Founder-led sales, community, content
- **Growth** (100-1000 customers): Paid channels, partnerships, SEO at scale
- **Scale** (1000+ customers): Brand, PR, enterprise motions, international

### 3. Idea Scoring Framework
Rate ideas against your situation.

**Scoring dimensions** (1-5 each):
- **Reach**: Audience size you can access
- **Relevance**: Fit to your ICP
- **Effort**: Time/resources required (lower = better)
- **Uniqueness**: Differentiation from competitors
- **Compounding**: Does this get better over time?

**Top score = (Reach × Relevance) / Effort**

### 4. Quick Start for Each Idea
Every idea includes a 3-step quick start.

**Example — Subreddit Marketing**:
1. Find 3 subreddits where ICP hangs out (tool: `gummy-search.com`)
2. Spend 2 weeks providing value (no promotion)
3. Post case study or tool after credibility built

## Prerequisites

- Clear definition of ICP and product value proposition
- Known growth stage
- Team size and bandwidth estimate

## Best Practices

**1. Test before scaling**
Run a 2-week experiment for any new channel before committing budget.

**2. Focus beats breadth**
Pick 3 channels maximum and do them well. Most success comes from mastery, not coverage.

**3. Steal from adjacent markets**
If you're SaaS, look what works in consumer apps. Adapt, don't copy.

## Common Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Idea Sprint for Q2 Planning
**Scenario**: Marketing team needs 10 ideas to evaluate for Q2 roadmap.

**Workflow**:
1. Filter library by stage (Growth) and budget (mid-range)
2. Score top 20 ideas on 5-dimension framework
3. Select top 10 by score
4. Assign each to team member for 1-week feasibility research
5. Choose 3 to pilot in Q2

### Use Case 2: "We've Tried Everything" Reset
**Scenario**: Team in plateau, need novel ideas outside current playbook.

**Workflow**:
1. List all tactics currently running
2. Filter idea library to exclude running tactics
3. Sort by "Uniqueness" score descending
4. Pick top 5 most novel ideas with reasonable Effort score

## Troubleshooting

**Issue**: Ideas feel generic, not tailored to product
**Solution**: Apply ICP filter before browsing. Ask: "Does this idea naturally reach [specific persona]?"

**Issue**: Team keeps returning to same 5 tactics
**Solution**: Block out 1 hour to explore ideas outside top-3 channels. Set a rule: one experiment from an untested channel per quarter.
